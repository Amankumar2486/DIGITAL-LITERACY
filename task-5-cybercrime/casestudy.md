Case Study: Ransomware Attack

Ransomware is a type of cybercrime where attackers block access to a victim’s data or system and demand payment (usually in cryptocurrency) to restore access. A well-known example is the WannaCry ransomware attack, which affected thousands of computers worldwide.

In a typical ransomware attack, the process begins when a user unknowingly downloads a malicious file. This often happens through phishing emails, fake software updates, or unsafe websites. Once the file is opened, the ransomware installs itself silently in the system. It then encrypts important files such as documents, images, and databases, making them inaccessible. After encryption, a message appears on the screen demanding a ransom payment within a limited time, often threatening permanent data loss if not paid.

Ransomware attacks usually target individuals, small businesses, hospitals, and large organizations. These targets are chosen because they rely heavily on their data and may be more willing to pay quickly to restore operations.

The consequences of ransomware attacks can be severe. Victims may lose critical data permanently, face financial loss due to ransom payments, and suffer reputational damage. In organizations like hospitals, such attacks can even disrupt essential services, putting lives at risk. Additionally, paying the ransom does not guarantee data recovery, making prevention and cybersecurity awareness extremely important.
